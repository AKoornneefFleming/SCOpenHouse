Project Introduction

Southern African Wildlife College Campus Basemap, Navigation, Asset Management and StoryMaps

Image from SAWC home page.

Client Background

Southern African Wildlife College (SAWC) has been proudly operating for 26 years with the aim of meeting training needs for natural resource management. Situated within Kruger National Park, SAWC offers a variety of educational services on the topics of conservation, wildlife management, anti-poaching, and community development. The school also offers supporting services that are integral to the management of the park system itself (SAWC Annual Report, 2021).

SAWC provides training on wildlife management, nature-based tourism, community-based natural resource management, responsible resource use, anti-poaching skills, and other environmental subjects (SAWC Annual Report, 2021). They have trained over 20,000 students, including more than 14,000 field rangers, and 1,200 natural resource managers from over 56 countries and 127 wildlife areas (SAWC website).

The college has experimented with and successfully integrated geographic information systems in several projects, incorporating the technology into their Applied Technology and Learning Unit. They are interested in further advancing these capabilities with an organized database and basemap, as well as establishing enterprise technology solutions to support various departments in the organization, such as Marketing and Operations

Problem Statement

Ideal:

A cartographically pleasing basemap and application solutions that support on and off campus navigation, life safety, asset management, marketing material and relevant research projects. These should be built upon a well-organized data structure. These solutions should be reliable enough to adapt to both online and offline scenarios where applicable, and straightforward enough to be maintained and taught by campus staff in support of the existing longitudinal studies.

Reality:

The only existing basemaps for the campus are rudimentary and lacking detail. Current GIS datasets require further development and organization. There are no significant digital navigation solutions available, with or without resilience to load shedding.

Consequences:

It is difficult for visitors to navigate around the campus, and they often get lost. Additional GIS work is difficult to develop without sufficient basemap or operational layers, and services are lost during load shedding. There is significant risk of information loss regarding critical infrastructure, and it would be difficult to identify or communicate the location of life safety equipment or assets during an emergency.

Proposal:

Collect and organize sufficient data to support the development of a basemap and operational layers, that can be integrated into online and offline solutions that meet the client’s use-cases.

Project Summary

Safari Consulting, worked with Southern African Wildlife College to learn about and fulfill their current objectives, by leveraging GIS technologies to deliver quality geospatial solutions. The seven deliverable solutions include data acquisition, file geodatabase development, custom basemaps, ArcGIS StoryMaps templates, navigation solutions, asset management solutions, and GIS instruction packages. These deliverables were built over a six-week period, with a one-week phase of data acquisition and a five-week phase of project development. These solutions will ultimately help the college in a variety of meaningful ways including (but not limited to) data organization and management, visualization of the college and their research areas, asset management, and establishing a foundation for future their GIS projects.
