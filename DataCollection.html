Data Collection

Overview

Priority:

Required

Objectives:

Support Operations and EM, Promote SAWC using GIS, Campus Navigation, Online/Offline Data Accessibility

Academic Requirements:

Data acquisition, database design

Additional Details:

An efficient strategy for on-site data collection was imperative to the success of the collaborative project, as every deliverable is dependent on the availability of quality spatial data.

Methodology

To maximize time and effort on location, the team created feature classes for data collection, hosted as ArcGIS web maps (web map) in AGOL and accessed through ArcGIS Field Maps (Field Maps). Domains, Field Map surveys, and offline map extents were created to help streamline collection while in the field, even during the frequent power outages and loss of network service. In some cases, existing spatial data provided by the client while Safari Consulting was on-site was also added to the web map, for in-field verification (e.g. fire extinguishers, buildings and rooms).

ArcGIS Pro Feature Class attribute table, domain and fields table.

A second component to data acquisitions was the organization and review of the existing spatial data, which was provided by the client upon our on-site arrival. Data existed in a myriad of locations and formats. The scope and types of available raw data needed to be interpreted and understood before it could be cleaned and incorporated into the rest of the project deliverables.

Results

The use of offline web maps in Field Maps, with the generic data collection layers and applicable provided spatial data included, helped the team collect new records and verify existing records across the 83-acre campus Some in-field collection took place with members of the SAWC staff to locate and contextualize points of interest, and to better understand the use-case for data. Sufficient spatial data was gathered from the client and cleaned to support the remaining deliverables.

Various points of data collected across the campus using ArcGIS Field Maps, displayed overtop Esri's standard imagery basemap.
